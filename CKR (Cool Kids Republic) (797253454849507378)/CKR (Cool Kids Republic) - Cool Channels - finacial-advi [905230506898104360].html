Howdy everyone! Time for some financial advice with Dragon, today we'll be discussing what it takes to get your first job! (Keep in mind that my advice will be mainly limited to the U.S, as that is where I currently live. There are similar systems in other countries) So the first step to employment will be getting a Worker's Permit, or Youth Employment Certificate (Only required if you are under the age of 18, yet most people within this server fit that category). Most if not all U.S state governments have websites dedicated to this explaining the specifics, where you can learn about your work hour restrictions as a minor, employer requirements, and what forums you need a legal guardian's signature on. The next part will be finding a job, and with minimal experience, this may be difficult. As a minor, the two best things you can do to improve your resume is volunteer work, ideally with a letter of recommendation from which ever organisation you are working with, as well as adequate grades in whichever school you are attending or have previously attended. The most common jobs for underage works in the U.S are in hospitality and retail, yet jobs can also be found a public library s or intern positions at many offices. Once you get a job, you'll need to announce your income to the IRS, for this you'll use a W-4. A W-4 is tax forum which and find on the IRS website, and you'll need to file it either before your first day working or before your first paycheck. This forum is what you will use to decide how much money your employer should withhold from your paycheck. Keep in mind that there are criteria to the amount of taxes you pay, and that if you pay more than required, you will be reimbursed in the form of a tax refund, and if you pay less, you will be required by the IRS to pay up. A W-2 form, which is provided by your employer, will be quite useful in determine your tax rate. The IRS website can provide more information on this topic, yet I find that other websites such as nerdwallet can also provide good info. Do keep in mind though that minors will have less restrictions on their taxes, and depending on their income may not pay any at all. (edited)
